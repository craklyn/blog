ATLAS Detector Overview

Some basic information about the ATLAS detector. This will help to motivate my research.

The Standard Model

Scientists' job is to understand and describe the world around us. Such an explanation is simply called a Theory. A theory that is limited in scope is an effective theory. The Standard Model is the effective theory of high energy particle physics. It correctly accounts for all measurements made in particle colliders to date, and describes all of the known particles.


So the Standard Model accurately describes all observations made by colliders to date. Why do I add the caveat that it's only an effective theory? The answer is because we know there is more physics out there, waiting to be discovered. Astronomers have observed that there is other matter in the univesre causing strong gravitation within and between galaxies. This matter is not observed directly, so it is referred to as dark matter. Presumably, dark matter is made up of new particles that have not been discovered yet. There are also some theoretical problems with the Standard Model, such as the ominously-named hierarchy problem.
